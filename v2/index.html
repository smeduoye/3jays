How we begin

Prototype first. Train alongside. Build only what proves itself.

Most AI programmes start with decks and end with disappointment. Engagement models

Four ways we work, shaped by twenty years in enterprise delivery.

Engagements typically start with a discovery sprint or executive briefing. Some move into platform build. Others focus on leadership readiness across the C-suite and senior management. Each model has a defined shape so you know what you're committing to.

Platform delivery

AI Platform Sprint

Two to four weeks · one workflow · fixed scope

For teams with a real operational problem who need to see a working next-stage system before committing to build.

  • Current-state workflow and integration map
  • Working prototype with LLM or automation where justified
  • Architecture, compliance and rollout notes
  • Go / no-go recommendation with costed path forward
Platform delivery

Enterprise AI Build

Fixed scope · 8–16 weeks · agreed milestones

For prototypes that prove value. We deliver production-grade platforms: data models, deterministic rules, integrations and the right level of AI.

  • Integration with existing front, middle and back-office systems
  • Deployment under human oversight with BAU handover

How we work

Three principles that decide what we build and what we teach.

— 01

Deterministic core, thin AI layer

Decisions that matter — pricing, compliance, matching — belong in software. AI handles parsing, drafting and pattern recognition where it earns its place.

— 02

Leaders and platforms together

Training without delivery produces slides. Delivery without leadership produces shelf-ware. We advance both so adoption survives the launch.

— 03

Accuracy before scale

Prototype until it's right. Ship narrow, accurate systems first. Auditability, supportability and governance are deliberate — not afterthoughts.
